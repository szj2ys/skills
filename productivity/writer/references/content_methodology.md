## 1. Topic Selection Methodology

### The Topic Intersection Model

A good topic = your area of expertise + broad reader interest + the current moment

Pure expertise can be too niche. Pure reader interest can be shallow. Pure trend-chasing can be fleeting. Only by combining all three can you find the precise trigger point.

### Topic Sources

**X (Twitter)**: Search the "AI" keyword daily for first-hand intel; follow AI bloggers and company updates
**Reddit**: r/artificial, r/ChatGPT, r/singularity, r/ClaudeAI, etc. (100+ upvotes = reasonably hot)
**Xiaohongshu**: The AI discourse pulse of mainstream public opinion — pay close attention to comment sections
**Jike**: AI bloggers, product ideas, industry insights
**Weibo / Douyin / Bilibili**: Trending topics — it only counts as truly "crossing over" if it's hot on at least two or three platforms simultaneously
**Reader communities**: The pain points, struggles, and recurring needs of community members. Monitor keywords like "anyone know," "recommend," etc.

### The HKR Quality Check

Before writing, every topic must pass all three tests:
- **H (Happy)**: Is it interesting enough? Does it have suspense? Can the title and opening make someone curious enough to click?
- **K (Knowledge)**: Is there substance? Will readers walk away having learned something new?
- **R (Resonance)**: Does it hit an emotional nerve? Will people think "yes, exactly, I've felt the same way"?

An S-tier topic nails all three. Passable requires at least two. If it only scores on one, reconsider. If it hits zero, kill it without hesitation.

### Role-Stepping Method (Divergent Thinking)

When evaluating a topic, step into different roles:
- "A busy everyday user": Can it save me time or solve a concrete problem?
- "A friend who loves cool stuff": Is it fun enough to make me go "wait, you can do that?!"
- "An anxious learner": Can I pick up something new or see things from a fresh angle?

## 2. Topic Categories and Past Viral Examples

### By Domain

**Large Model Technology** (LLMs, algorithms, agents, coding)
- GPT-5.2 dropped, and it's the AI that blue-collar workers have been waiting for.
- After two weeks with the Doubao phone, I feel like I've been caught in a war between old and new.
- After deliberately poisoning AI, I think the entire internet has become a Dark Forest.
- RPA + AI is the real automation god-tier combo that'll let you coast.
- I used AI to monitor Sam Altman — whenever he tweets, my phone automatically calls me.

**AIGC Art** (AI design, video, music, digital avatars, creative play)
- The AI movie-set BTS trend that's blowing up — here's a step-by-step tutorial.
- Suno V5 triggered a full-on Renaissance across Bilibili.
- How was that AI video Jay Chou posted — the one with 14 million likes — actually made?
- Nano Banana went from zero to legendary overnight. Here are 10 pro-level tricks the official docs won't tell you.

**Broad Internet Culture** (deep dives, phenomenon stories, opinion pieces, policy analysis)
- The DeepSeek listings on Taobao for 9.9 yuan showed me a bizarre, folded-over world.
- After analyzing 180 million job postings, it looks like fresh graduates might be getting locked out by AI.
- A 17-year-old boy pretending to be AI somehow healed all of Bilibili.
- The world's digital refugees are flooding into Xiaohongshu.

### By Content Type

**Product Reviews + Recommendations**: Hands-on experience-driven, with clear likes and dislikes
**Playbook + Tutorial Sharing**: Step-by-step guides for readers, centered on prompts and procedures
**Phenomenon Interviews**: Internet phenomenon breakdowns, personality profiles
**Cognitive Opinions**: Deep analysis of events, research paper breakdowns
**AI Shenanigans**: Building fun things yourself, showing what AI can really do

### By Writing Archetype (Distilled from 8 Best-Performing Articles)

**Investigative Experiment**
You personally do the thing, reporting discoveries in real time. The strongest at building trust and narrative tension.
- DeepSeek for 9.9 yuan on Taobao → bought it, discovered they just sent free URLs
- Deliberately poisoning AI → registered a fake account, posted false notes, watched AI get corrupted step by step
- Paid 499 yuan for door-to-door OpenClaw installation → sent someone to actually experience the service
Core technique: process narration + layer-by-layer discovery + authentic reactions at every step

**Product Experience**
Get your hands on a product and use it, bringing the reader along for the ride. Not a review report — it's "come play with me."
- miclaw phone agent → starting from setting up the persona, demoing features one by one
- Seedance 2.0 → organized by use case, each paired with a real example
Core technique: scenario-driven + your genuine feelings + natural comparisons with other products

**Phenomenon Analysis**
Spot something interesting, then dig in layer by layer.
- Three-panel image goes viral → asking why it blew up → connecting to black-border photos from ten years ago → life narrative needs
- AI can't see a heart → testing each model → finding the research paper → temporal blindness → Gestalt psychology → philosophical insight
Core technique: curiosity-driven → research → knowledge drops → philosophical elevation

**Tool Sharing**
Share a useful tool or prompt, but wrap it in a personal story. Never just "here's a great tool you should try."
- The talent discovery prompt → starting from a friend's anxiety → reflecting on the meaning of "talent" → showing the tool → sharing your own test results
Core technique: personal story setup → naturally introducing the tool → walking through your own usage → showing the results

## 3. Content Rhythm

Rhythm isn't about writing skill — it's a propulsion system that keeps readers scrolling.

Good rhythm works like a wave: each time you drift slightly off the main thread (letting the reader breathe, learn something, see an example), then pull them back with a single line and keep pushing forward.

**Main-thread anchor sentences** should appear frequently — they don't need to be long, one line is enough, but the frequency must be high.

**The cardinal sin**: Drifting far off-topic then yanking the reader back by force. They have to spend mental energy re-tracing your logic, and the flow state breaks.

Rhythm priority: Expertise (foundation) → Perspective (direction) → Main thread (rope) → Rhythm (pulling force)

**Reference creators**: Yingsufeng, He Tongxue, Xiaoshi Diary, Bi Dao, Xiao Lin, Jiu Bian

## 4. Creative Case Study Workflow

### Three Anchor Types

**Trending Anchor**: The hottest movies, shows, social events, internet memes right now — the largest common denominator for traffic
**Pain-Point Anchor**: The most frequent, most frustrating problems users face — practical value
**Showmanship Anchor**: The most mind-bending core capability of the AI tool itself — technical wow factor

### Packaging as a Micro-Story

1. Drop the "challenge": What problem are you trying to solve?
2. Reveal the "brain flash": What wild approach did you come up with?
3. Show the "process": How did you pull it off step by step? (screenshots, key prompts)
4. Detonate the "result": That final output that makes people go "holy sh*t"

**Guiding principle**: Every case must have standalone entertainment value, creative value, and inspirational value. If it doesn't get you hyped, don't publish it.
