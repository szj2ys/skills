# ByteDance Marketing Framework Reference

This reference expands the core models used in the ByteDance marketing playbook. Load it when the user needs deeper detail on FACT+, A1-A5, creator tiers, or platform mechanics.

---

## 1. FACT+ Marketing Scenes

FACT+ is the brand-effect framework used by Ocean Engine (巨量引擎) and Douyin/TikTok ecosystem teams. It is a portfolio of four scenes, not a funnel.

### F — Field 阵地自营 (Owned Field)
- **Purpose**: Build durable owned assets and repeat traffic
- **Tactics**: Official brand account, self-hosted live streams, mini-shop/Storefront, content series, search SEO inside the app, fan-group operation
- **Best for**: Mature products, high-frequency categories, long purchase cycles, retention/upsell
- **Key metrics**: Follower growth, live GMV, owned traffic share, repeat purchase rate, fan-to-buyer ratio

### A — Alliance 达人矩阵 (Creator Alliance)
- **Purpose**: Borrow trust and vertical expertise at scale
- **Tactics**: Tiered KOL/KOC matrix, seeding, creator co-branded content, affiliate/live distribution, employee advocacy
- **Best for**: New products needing proof, complex products needing explanation, emotional products needing storytelling
- **Key metrics**: Creator content volume, engagement rate, attributed GMV, CPE/CPV by tier, content-to-search lift

### C — Campaign 主题活动 (Campaign Events)
- **Purpose**: Create time-boxed spikes in attention or conversion
- **Tactics**: Platform mega-sales (618, 11.11), IP co-branding, hashtag challenges, limited drops, brand festivals
- **Best for**: Seasonal demand, new launches, inventory pushes, competitive windows
- **Key metrics**: Hashtag views, challenge participation, spike GMV, share of voice, new buyer share

### T — TopView 头部品效 (Premium Brand-Effect)
- **Purpose**: Mass awareness + immediate performance signal
- **Tactics**: Splash ads, feed takeovers, brand-effect bundled buys, premium placements, celebrity/IP fronting
- **Best for**: Brand repositioning, large launches, defending category share
- **Key metrics**: Brand search lift, ad recall, reach/frequency, direct response rate, halo ROAS

## 2. A1-A5 User Asset Model

The A1-A5 model describes the relationship depth between a user and a brand/product. Marketing should pick the weakest transition, not the largest stage.

| Stage | Chinese | Meaning | Content/Action Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | Aware 认知 | User knows the brand/product | Ad exposure, brand search, hashtag view |
| A2 | Appeal 兴趣 | User engages with content | Like, comment, follow, full video view, share |
| A3 | Ask 问询 | User actively considers | Search, Q&A, DM, live question, add-to-cart |
| A4 | Act 行动 | User converts | Purchase, download, sign-up, form submit |
| A5 | Advocate 拥护 | User repurchases and recommends | Repeat purchase, UGC, referral, positive review |

### Typical Intervention Patterns
- **A1→A2**: Viral hooks, mass reach, UGC challenges, creator drama
- **A2→A3**: Benefit demos, comparison content, creator explainers, search capture
- **A3→A4**: Live commerce, limited offers, social proof, risk reversal (trial/return policy)
- **A4→A5**: Membership, loyalty content, seeding community, referral rewards

## 3. Content Formats and Triggers

| Format | When It Works | Pitfalls |
|---|---|---|
| Hook demo (first 3 seconds) | A2→A3, functional products | Do not make it look like an ad; lead with the problem |
| Creator testimonial | A2→A3, trust-sensitive categories | Creator must feel native, not scripted |
| Live commerce | A3→A4, impulse/offer-driven categories | Requires real-time ops, inventory, and host training |
| UGC challenge | A1→A2, A5 advocacy | Keep participation cost extremely low |
| Story/mini-drama | A1 awareness, emotional categories | Story must still land the product benefit |
| Search / educational | A2→A3, complex products | Optimize for in-app search keywords |

## 4. Creator Tier Matrix

| Tier | Fan Range | Role | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mega | 10M+ | Mass awareness, cultural moment | T-scene launches, major brand events |
| Head | 1M-10M | Authority + reach | A-scene proof points, category education |
| Mid | 100K-1M | Vertical depth + conversion | A-scene main force, explainers, demos |
| Micro | 10K-100K | Niche trust, high engagement | A-scene seeding, proof volume |
| KOC / Employee | <10K or 0 | Authentic social proof | Reviews, real scenarios, internal advocacy |

### Selection Heuristics
- One mega/head creator can borrow category authority; too many dilute the message.
- Mid-tier creators usually deliver the best engagement-to-cost ratio for A2→A3.
- Micro/KOC creators create the volume of proof needed for A3→A4 trust.
- Always match creator vertical to product use-case, not just follower count.

## 5. Paid Distribution Mechanics

| Tool | Use Case | Key Setting |
|---|---|---|
| DOU+ / content heating | Scale proven organic content | Only boost content that already has positive early signals |
| Feed ads (信息流) | Reach + conversion at scale | Optimize for the chosen A-stage objective, not just clicks |
| Search ads / brand zone | Capture A3 intent | Bid on creator-name + product-benefit keywords |
| Live traffic ads | Drive viewers to live room | Match host energy to traffic pacing |
| TopView / splash | Brand launch / defense | Pair with a strong CTA or search prompt |

## 6. Differentiation from Competing Methodologies

| Platform | Core Logic | ByteDance Contrast |
|---|---|---|
| Kuaishou 快手 | Trust-driven community / "old buddy" culture | ByteDance is algorithm-first; creator trust is rented, not owned |
| Tencent 微信 | Social-chain private-domain | ByteDance is public-domain discovery; weaker private retention, stronger scale |
| Meta | Social graph + interest tags | ByteDance relies more on passive watch behavior than active likes/follows |
| Google | Search intent | ByteDance creates intent through content, rather than waiting for it |
| Taobao | Transaction field | ByteDance is content field first; commerce is a byproduct of attention |

Use this contrast to explain why a ByteDance-style campaign is appropriate for products where awareness or consideration must be built through content, rather than harvested from existing search/social demand.
